## Claude's Output:

### Why Small Dev Teams Are Outgrowing Linear

Linear is fast. It's beautiful. And if you're a 3-person startup sprinting toward product-market fit, it might be exactly what you need.

But something happens around team size 5 or 6. Suddenly the rigidity that made Linear feel clean starts to feel like a cage. You need a space for async documentation. You need your designer in the loop without giving them full engineer access. You need a view that isn't a sprint board.

Linear doesn't have that. By design.

The Linear team made a deliberate bet: optimize for speed and developer experience above all else. It's a great bet for them. But it means they're not building the flexible, multi-role workspace that small teams actually need as they grow.

Here's what we see happening: teams hit a wall around their first non-engineer hire. The PM, the designer, the growth person — they look at Linear and bounce. So the team ends up with Linear for engineering and Notion for everyone else. Two systems, two sources of truth, twice the overhead.

Small teams can't afford that split. You need one place that bends to your workflow, not the other way around.

That's the gap. And it's wide open.
